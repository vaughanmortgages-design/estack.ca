FindMyRate.ca Review: Compare Canadian Mortgage Rates Instantly

Updated July 2026  ·  6 min read  ·  estack.ca editorial team

Shopping for a mortgage used to mean calling your bank, waiting for a callback, and hoping the rate you were quoted was competitive. FindMyRate.ca changes that. It's a Canadian rate comparison platform that surfaces mortgage offers from multiple lenders in seconds — no appointment required.

This review covers how FindMyRate.ca works, who benefits most from using it, and how it stacks up against going directly to your bank.

What Is FindMyRate.ca?

FindMyRate.ca is an independent Canadian mortgage rate aggregator. You enter basic details about your purchase or refinance — property value, down payment, location, and credit profile — and the platform returns competitive rate offers from a curated network of lenders. The lenders include chartered banks, credit unions, and monoline mortgage lenders (lenders that focus exclusively on mortgages).

The site is free to use. FindMyRate.ca earns a referral fee if you proceed with a lender through their platform, which is standard practice for Canadian rate comparison tools. This doesn't affect the rate you're quoted.

Important distinction: FindMyRate.ca is a rate aggregator, not a mortgage broker. It shows you what's available. A licensed mortgage professional can then help you apply, negotiate, and structure the deal.

How the Rate Comparison Works

The process is straightforward. You fill in a short form covering your scenario — purchase price, down payment or equity position, property type, amortisation period, and term preference (fixed vs. variable). The platform then pulls current rate offerings from its lender network and displays them side by side.

Results are typically sorted by rate, but you can filter by term length, lender type, or whether the mortgage is open or closed. Each listing shows the advertised rate, the lender name, and a link to proceed. Some listings include monthly payment estimates based on your inputs, which makes it easier to compare the real-world cost of a slightly lower rate against one that comes with more flexible prepayment privileges.

Self-employed borrowers or those with complex credit situations should note that the lowest advertised rates on any comparison site are typically reserved for strong A-lender profiles — salaried employment, 20%+ down payment, 680+ credit score. If your situation is more nuanced, the rates shown are still useful as a benchmark, but you'll want professional guidance before drawing firm conclusions.

Tips for Getting the Lowest Rate

  1. Know your credit score before you start. Most lenders tier their rates by credit band. Knowing your score helps you assess which offers are actually accessible to you.
  2. Compare the same term type. A 5-year fixed and a 5-year variable are not apples-to-apples. Filter by the term you actually want.
  3. Read the prepayment privileges. A rate that's 0.15% lower but limits you to 10% annual prepayments may cost more than a slightly higher rate with 20% prepayments — depending on how aggressively you plan to pay down your mortgage.
  4. Look at the lender, not just the rate. Monoline lenders often post the sharpest rates, but some borrowers prefer the convenience of a lender with branch access or an existing banking relationship.
  5. Act on what you find. Rates shift daily with bond yields. If you see a rate that works for your budget, move quickly — comparison tools show a snapshot, not a guarantee.

FindMyRate.ca vs. Going Directly to Your Bank

Banks offer one product: their own. A comparison platform offers many. That's the core difference.

When you walk into your bank for a mortgage, the representative is obligated to show you their institution's products only. They can't tell you that a competing lender is offering a sharper rate, and they won't. FindMyRate.ca solves for that limitation by aggregating the market in one place.

One caveat: Not every lender in Canada is on every comparison platform. Some credit unions and regional lenders only distribute through brokers or direct channels. A comprehensive rate search should include both a comparison tool and a conversation with a broker who can access lenders not listed on any public-facing aggregator.

Bottom Line

FindMyRate.ca is a practical first step for any Canadian mortgage search. It's fast, free, and gives you a realistic picture of what the market looks like before you commit to any single lender. Use it to benchmark your options — especially at renewal, when your bank is counting on inertia to keep you from shopping around.

For borrowers with straightforward files, the platform can take you most of the way to a competitive rate. For more complex situations, treat it as research that makes your follow-up conversations more productive.
