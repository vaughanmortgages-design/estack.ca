How to Get the Best Deals

  • Add items to your wishlist now. Amazon shows price history and alerts you when wishlisted items go on sale.
  • Check deals early. The best Prime Day deals sell out fast — especially in Canada where inventory is lower than the US.
  • Compare with other retailers. Best Buy Canada and Canadian Tire often price-match during Prime Day week.
  • Watch Lightning Deals. These are time-limited, quantity-limited offers. Check back every few hours.
  • Prime membership required. Non-Prime deals exist but the best prices are members-only. The $9.99/month membership pays for itself in one good deal.
Pro tip: Use the Amazon Canada app and turn on deal notifications for your wishlisted items. Lightning Deals go live without warning and sell out in minutes.

Is It Worth It for Canadian Homeowners?

For most categories — yes. Tools, smart home, and appliances consistently see their best annual prices during Prime Day. The caveat: not every "deal" is a real discount. Use a price tracker like CamelCamelCamel to verify the discount is genuine before buying.
