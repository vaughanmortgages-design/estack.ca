How to Get More Leads as a Self-Employed Tradesperson

estack.caJune 20267 min read

Word of mouth is great — until it isn't. Every tradesperson hits the same wall: the referrals slow down, a big client wraps up, and suddenly the pipeline looks thin. The solution isn't working harder. It's building a system that generates leads while you're on the job.

Why Most Tradespeople Struggle With Lead Gen

It's not the trade — it's the marketing. Most contractors are exceptional at their craft and terrible at self-promotion. The good news: you don't need to become a marketer. You need a handful of tools and a consistent system.

1. Your Website is a Lead Machine — If You Set It Up Right

A basic website with a contact form isn't enough in 2026. You need lead capture that works automatically. LeadScout identifies visitors to your site and turns anonymous traffic into real contact information — without the visitor filling out a form.

Turn Your Website Traffic Into Leads

2. Google Business Profile — Free and Underused

If you're a trades business serving a local area, your Google Business Profile is the most valuable free marketing tool available. A complete profile with photos and reviews can generate more leads than a paid ad campaign. Update it monthly, respond to every review, and post project photos consistently.

3. Build a Simple Follow-Up System

Most tradespeople lose jobs not because they lost the bid — but because they never followed up. A simple CRM or even a spreadsheet tracking every quote, with a reminder to follow up in 3 days and 7 days, will increase your close rate significantly.

4. Collect Reviews Systematically

After every completed job, ask the client directly for a Google review. Make it easy — text them the link. Five genuine reviews will outperform any ad you can run for local search visibility.
