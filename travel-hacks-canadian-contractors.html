Travel Hacks for Canadian Contractors & Tradespeople

estack.caJune 20265 min read

Whether you're heading south for a work contract, taking a vacation between jobs, or managing a project across the border — Canadian contractors and tradespeople have unique travel needs. Here's how to travel smarter and cheaper.

The Biggest Travel Mistake Canadians Make

Roaming fees. Most Canadians still travel with their domestic SIM and get hit with $15–20/day in roaming charges. That's $150+ on a 10-day trip for something that's completely avoidable in 2026.

Track Business Travel Expenses Properly

If you're travelling for a job or contract, most of your travel expenses are deductible under CRA — flights, accommodation, vehicle costs, and meals (50%). Keep every receipt. Use a dedicated business card or prepaid card for travel expenses to simplify categorization at tax time.

US Work Contracts — What You Need to Know

Canadian contractors taking US contracts need a TN visa or appropriate work authorization. CAD/USD conversion also matters — lock in rates when the Canadian dollar strengthens, and invoice in USD when possible to protect your margin.

Travel Insurance for the Self-Employed

If you don't have group benefits, travel insurance is a must. Look for plans that cover emergency medical, trip cancellation, and equipment if you're bringing tools across the border.
