How to Start a Shopify Store in Canada (2026)

estack.caJune 20267 min read

Canada is one of the best markets in the world for ecommerce. Strong consumer spending, high internet penetration, and a growing preference for buying from Canadian brands. Shopify — a Canadian company — makes it straightforward to launch without a tech background.

Step 1 — Sign Up and Pick a Plan

Shopify's free trial lets you build and test your store before you pay anything. Start with the Basic plan at $38 CAD/month — it covers everything you need to launch and start selling.

Step 2 — Set Up Canadian Taxes

This trips up a lot of new store owners. In Canada, tax rates vary by province — GST, HST, or PST depending on where your customer is located. Shopify handles this automatically if you configure it correctly. Go to Settings → Taxes → Canada and enable automatic tax calculation.

Step 3 — Activate Shopify Payments

Shopify Payments is available in Canada and eliminates third-party transaction fees. You'll need your business banking info and a Canadian address. Once activated, you accept Visa, Mastercard, Apple Pay and Google Pay out of the box.

Step 4 — Configure Shipping

Set up domestic Canadian shipping rates and optionally US/international. Canada Post integration is built in. For high volume operations, look at apps like Shipbob or ShipStation for rate shopping.

Step 5 — Add Products and Launch

Keep it simple at launch. 5–10 products, clean photos, clear descriptions. Don't wait for perfect. You can iterate once you have real traffic and customer feedback.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the Canadian tax setup — you'll owe CRA eventually
  • Launching with too many products and no clear focus
  • Not building an email list from day one
  • Underpricing — always factor in COGS, shipping, and platform fees
