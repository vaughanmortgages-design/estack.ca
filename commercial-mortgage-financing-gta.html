Commercial Mortgage Financing in the GTA: A Practical Guide

Updated June 2026  ·  9 min read  ·  estack.ca editorial team

Commercial mortgages run on different rules than residential ones — different underwriting, different documentation, and a much wider range of lenders and structures. If you're buying a mixed-use building in Vaughan, refinancing a plaza in Brampton, or financing a small industrial unit in Concord, the process looks nothing like getting approved for a house.

Here's how it actually works in the GTA market.

What Counts as a Commercial Mortgage

Generally, anything that isn't a standard 1–4 unit residential property falls into commercial financing:

  • Mixed-use buildings (retail/commercial ground floor with residential above)
  • Multi-unit residential (5+ units)
  • Retail plazas and strip malls
  • Office buildings and medical/professional space
  • Industrial and warehouse properties
  • Land for development or holding

How Commercial Underwriting Differs

Residential lenders look mostly at your personal income and credit. Commercial lenders look mostly at the property's ability to pay for itself — the net operating income (NOI) relative to the debt service. The key metric is the Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR): how much income the property generates compared to what the mortgage payment costs.

Why this matters for buyers with variable income: Because commercial lenders weight the property's own income heavily, a strong-performing property can sometimes get financed even when the buyer's personal income documentation isn't perfect — the opposite dynamic from a residential mortgage.

What Lenders Want to See

  1. Net operating income (NOI) — rent roll, lease terms, and operating expenses for the property
  2. Tenant quality and lease length — a building with strong, long-term tenants underwrites better than one with month-to-month leases
  3. Property condition — environmental reports, structural assessments, and a Phase I environmental site assessment are common requirements
  4. Borrower/guarantor financials — still matters, especially for owner-occupied or smaller deals
  5. Exit and refinance strategy — commercial terms are shorter, so lenders want to know your plan at renewal

CMHC-Insured Commercial Options

For multi-unit residential properties (5+ units), CMHC offers insured commercial financing that can allow for higher leverage and longer amortizations than a conventional commercial mortgage — often the single biggest lever for improving cash flow on an apartment building deal. It comes with its own application process and energy/affordability requirements that are worth understanding before you commit to a purchase timeline.

GTA-Specific Considerations

Commercial real estate in the Greater Toronto Area carries its own dynamics worth factoring into any financing plan:

  • Zoning and permitted use vary significantly by municipality — Vaughan, Markham, and Brampton each have different processes for change-of-use or development applications
  • Land transfer tax applies to commercial purchases the same as residential, and Toronto proper layers on its own municipal LTT
  • Cap rates for GTA commercial assets vary widely by asset class and submarket — a number worth getting from a current appraisal, not a generic online source
Common mistake: Buyers shop for the property first and financing second, then discover the deal doesn't pencil out under commercial lending math. Getting a financing read on a property — even informally — before you're deep into negotiations saves a lot of wasted time.

Choosing the Right Lender for a Commercial Deal

Not every lender offers commercial mortgage products, and the ones that do vary significantly in how they assess risk, what property types they'll finance, and what terms they'll offer. Banks, credit unions, and private lenders each occupy different parts of the market — and the right fit depends on the property type, your income structure, and your timeline. Understanding lender appetite before you apply saves time and avoids unnecessary credit inquiries.

Bottom Line

Commercial mortgage financing in the GTA rewards preparation — clean financials on the property, a realistic view of DSCR, and a lender strategy that matches the asset type. Get the financing conversation started early, ideally before you're under contract, so the numbers and the deal stay aligned.
