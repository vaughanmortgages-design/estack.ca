Transparency & Standards

Editorial Policy

Last updated: June 2026  ·  info@estack.ca

estack.ca publishes independent financial content for self-employed Canadians, contractors, and small business owners. This page explains how we select topics, review products, disclose commercial relationships, and maintain editorial independence.

Short version: We earn commissions when you use affiliate links on this site. This never influences what we review, how we score it, or what we recommend. If a product isn't good for our audience, we say so — regardless of whether it pays us.

How We Choose What to Cover

Our editorial calendar is driven by reader needs, not partner relationships. Topics are selected based on:

  • Questions self-employed Canadians are actually searching for
  • Rate and policy changes that affect our audience's finances
  • Products and services we have personally evaluated or researched in depth
  • Gaps in Canadian financial content that leave our audience underserved

We do not accept paid placement in our editorial rankings or "best of" lists. Products earn their position on performance, not payments.

How We Review Products

For financial products — loans, savings accounts, brokerages, and tools — we evaluate:

  • Eligibility for self-employed applicants — our primary audience
  • True cost — rate, fees, hidden charges, and promotional vs. ongoing pricing
  • Customer experience — based on publicly available reviews and direct testing where possible
  • Regulatory standing — we verify licensing with FSRA, OSFI, and provincial regulators
  • Accessibility — can someone with variable income or a non-traditional credit profile actually get approved?

Affiliate Relationships & Disclosure

estack.ca earns commissions from some of the products and services we cover. These relationships help fund the site and allow us to publish free content. Current affiliate relationships include:

  • LeadScout — personal loan matching network (we earn a referral fee when users submit applications)
  • Vaughan Mortgage Group — we may earn a referral fee for mortgage clients we connect with Paul Malandrino
  • Questrade — brokerage referral program
  • Shopify — eCommerce platform affiliate
  • Windsor.ai — marketing analytics affiliate

Affiliate links are not hidden. Where a link leads to a partner product, it is because we believe that product is the best available for our audience — not because it pays the most.

Sponsored Content

estack.ca occasionally publishes sponsored articles written in partnership with financial brands. Sponsored content is always:

  • Clearly labelled at the top of the article with a "Sponsored" or "Partner Content" tag
  • Reviewed by our editorial team for accuracy before publication
  • Subject to our standard factual standards — we will not publish sponsored content that we believe to be misleading

Sponsors do not influence adjacent editorial content, ratings, or recommendations in non-sponsored articles.

Accuracy & Corrections

We make every effort to ensure rates, figures, and regulatory information are accurate at time of publication. Canadian financial markets move quickly — rates and product terms change.

If you spot an error, email us at info@estack.ca with the subject line "Editorial Correction." We review all requests and publish corrections when warranted, typically within 24 hours.

Not Financial Advice

estack.ca is an independent editorial publication. Our content is for informational purposes only and does not constitute financial, investment, tax, or legal advice. We are not registered financial advisors or mortgage brokers.

Decisions about loans, mortgages, investments, and business finances should be made in consultation with licensed professionals. For mortgage advice, we refer readers to Paul Malandrino at Vaughan Mortgage Group (FSRA #M25000307), a licensed mortgage agent.
