If you're running paid ads across Google, Meta, and TikTok simultaneously — and you have no idea which one is actually driving sales — Windsor.ai was built for that problem.

For Canadian small business owners and self-employed operators who are spending real money on digital ads, attribution is one of the biggest blind spots. You boost a Facebook post, run a Google search campaign, maybe throw some spend at TikTok — and then you look at your sales and have no idea which channel did the work.

Windsor.ai is a marketing analytics platform that pulls data from all your ad channels into one place, applies attribution modelling, and tells you which campaigns are actually generating revenue. Here's what we found.

What Windsor.ai Actually Does

Windsor.ai connects to your ad platforms — Google Ads, Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn, Shopify, WooCommerce, and 50+ others — and centralizes your marketing data into a single dashboard or data destination (Google Sheets, BigQuery, Looker Studio, etc.).

The core product has two main functions:

1. Data Connectors

Pull raw ad performance data from every platform into one place. No more logging into five dashboards. Windsor.ai acts as the pipeline — connecting your ad accounts to wherever you want to analyze the data.

2. Attribution Modelling

This is where it gets interesting. Windsor.ai applies AI-driven attribution to figure out which touchpoints in a customer's journey actually contributed to a conversion — not just the last click. For businesses running multi-channel campaigns, this matters a lot.

Who It's For

Windsor.ai is best suited for:

  • → Small to mid-size businesses running ads on 2+ platforms simultaneously
  • → E-commerce operators on Shopify or WooCommerce
  • → Marketing agencies managing multiple client accounts
  • → Anyone who wants their ad data in Google Sheets or Looker Studio
  • → Businesses frustrated with platform-reported ROAS that doesn't match actual revenue

It's probably overkill if you're only running one ad channel with a small budget. But once you're juggling Meta + Google + any e-commerce platform, the attribution problem gets real fast.

What We Like

The connector library is extensive — 50+ integrations covering essentially every major ad platform, CRM, and e-commerce tool. Setup is straightforward: connect your accounts, choose a data destination, and Windsor.ai handles the rest.

The AI attribution feature is the real differentiator. Most small businesses rely on last-click attribution by default — meaning they credit the last ad a customer clicked before buying. Windsor.ai's model looks at the full path, which typically reveals that your awareness campaigns (often Meta) are doing more work than last-click suggests.

The Google Sheets integration is particularly useful for Canadian SMBs who aren't ready to invest in a full BI tool — you get live ad data flowing into a spreadsheet you can customize however you want.

What to Consider

Windsor.ai is a data tool, not a campaign management platform. It won't optimize your ads, write copy, or suggest bid adjustments. It shows you what's happening — you still need to act on that data yourself.

For very small businesses with limited ad budgets (under $500/month total), the professional plan cost may outweigh the benefit. The attribution insights are most valuable when you have enough spend across enough channels to make the data meaningful.
