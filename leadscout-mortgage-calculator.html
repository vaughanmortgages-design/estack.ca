July 18, 2026  ·  6 min read  ·  estack.ca editorial team

Shopping for a mortgage rate in Canada often means sitting through a bank appointment, answering two dozen questions, and waiting 24 hours for a number you could have found online in a minute. LeadScout is a Canadian mortgage rate tool built to short-circuit that process — giving borrowers a real rate estimate in under 60 seconds, with no credit check and no sales pressure.

Here's what it does, who it helps most, and how it stacks up against going direct to your bank.

What Is LeadScout?

LeadScout is a Canadian mortgage marketplace and rate calculator. It connects borrowers with licensed Canadian lenders and brokers by collecting a handful of basic details about the property, purchase price, income type, and down payment — then instantly surfacing rate options from its lender network.

The tool operates online, is available 24/7, and requires no credit bureau pull at the initial inquiry stage. You get a rate range or specific options to explore, then choose whether to proceed with a full application.

How it works in brief: Enter your purchase price, location, income type, and down payment. LeadScout matches your profile to its lender network and surfaces the rate options you're most likely to qualify for — in about 60 seconds.

How the LeadScout Calculator Works

The calculator is designed to be simple and non-intimidating. The typical flow:

  1. Property details — you enter the property type (home, condo, rental), estimated purchase price, and province.
  2. Income type — this is where LeadScout diverges from most rate sites. You select whether you're salaried, self-employed, contract, or have other income.
  3. Down payment — amount and source (savings, gift, equity).
  4. Credit profile — a general range, not a hard pull. This helps filter for realistic options.
  5. Instant results — rate options populate from the network. You can explore them, compare terms, and initiate contact with a lender if interested.

The whole process takes roughly 60 seconds for the initial output. You can then dig deeper into specific lenders, terms, and payment estimates.

Why It's Especially Useful for Self-Employed and Non-Traditional Borrowers

Banks run their online rate calculators with a simple assumption baked in: that you're salaried, have two years of employment history, and are filing taxes as an employee. That's not the reality for millions of Canadians who are self-employed, contract workers, commission earners, or recently transitioned from employee to business owner.

LeadScout's income-type field matters because it routes your profile toward lenders who actually understand your income structure. Rather than showing you a bank rate you can't achieve, the results skew toward brokers and lenders who work with alternative documentation — business bank statements, GST returns, or stated income with strong equity.

LeadScout vs. Going Direct to Your Bank

The traditional path is calling your bank, booking an appointment, and receiving one rate from one lender who has one set of products. That rate may or may not be their best offer — and you have no benchmark to know either way.

Using a rate comparison tool like LeadScout gives you:

  • A baseline — you know roughly what's available before you walk into any conversation
  • Multiple lender types — banks, credit unions, B lenders, and broker channels in one place
  • No commitment — browsing rates doesn't obligate you to anything or touch your credit file
  • Time savings — minutes online versus days of appointments and callbacks
Note on accuracy: Online rate tools show estimates based on the profile you enter. The actual rate you're offered after a full underwritten application may differ based on your complete credit file, income verification, and property appraisal. Treat the calculator output as a starting point for comparison, not a guaranteed offer.

Tips for Getting the Best Result From the Calculator

  • Be accurate with your income type. Selecting "salaried" when you're self-employed will return rates you can't achieve. Match your income description to reality.
  • Use a realistic purchase price. The tool calculates your LTV and debt ratios based on what you enter — accurate inputs give useful outputs.
  • Know your down payment source. A gifted down payment from a family member is allowed but may affect which lenders will work with you — the tool accounts for this.
  • Check both fixed and variable. Run the calculator for both rate types and compare total cost over your planned term before deciding.
