CMI Financial Group Review: Canada's Largest Private Mortgage Lender

July 20, 2026  ·  8 min read  ·  estack.ca editorial team

If a bank or credit union has turned you down for a mortgage, you're not out of options. Canada has a growing private lending sector, and CMI Financial Group sits at the top of it. Established in 2005, CMI has grown into one of the most well-known private mortgage lenders in the country, serving borrowers across Ontario and major markets who don't fit the standard lending mould.

This review covers what CMI is, who qualifies, how their rates compare to traditional lenders, and what to expect if you apply.

What Is CMI Financial Group?

CMI Financial Group is a private mortgage lender and mortgage investment corporation (MIC) operating primarily in Ontario. Unlike banks, which are regulated federally and lend from deposits, CMI lends from a pool of private investor capital. This structure gives them significantly more flexibility in who they approve and how they structure loans.

CMI focuses on short-term mortgages — typically 12 months — intended as a bridge while a borrower improves their situation or waits to qualify at a traditional lender. Their core message is that they lend on the property's value and the overall story, not just a credit score and two years of T4s.

Who CMI typically serves: Self-employed borrowers with complex income, people with past credit issues (consumer proposals, collections, prior bankruptcy), short employment history, rental property investors, and those needing quick closes that banks can't accommodate.

CMI Mortgage Rates vs. Banks

Private mortgage rates are higher than bank rates — that's the trade-off for accessibility. Here's how they typically compare:

CMI's rates vary depending on loan-to-value (LTV), property type, location, and the borrower's overall risk profile. Borrowers with strong equity (below 70% LTV) and decent income documentation will see lower rates within that range. Those with thin documentation or previous credit events may be at the higher end.

Cost to consider: Private mortgages also carry lender fees, typically 1–2% of the loan amount, plus standard legal and brokerage costs. Factor the total cost into your plan, not just the interest rate.

Who CMI Will Lend To

CMI's lending criteria are substantially more flexible than a bank's. They consider:

  • Self-employed borrowers who write off significant business expenses and show low net income on paper
  • Borrowers with bruised credit — past missed payments, collections, or discharged bankruptcy (typically 2+ years out)
  • Consumer proposal borrowers — both during and after the proposal period
  • Short employment history — new Canadians, career changers, recently self-employed
  • Real estate investors needing quick closes or bridge financing
  • Non-conforming properties — rural, mixed-use, or properties that bank appraisers flag

The property itself carries significant weight in the decision. CMI will typically lend up to 75–80% LTV on urban properties and less on rural or semi-rural real estate. Your equity position is your strongest qualifying tool when income documentation is thin.

CMI Loan Products

CMI offers first and second mortgages across several categories:

  • First mortgages — typically up to 75–80% LTV on residential properties
  • Second mortgages — behind an existing first, up to 80% combined LTV
  • Bridge financing — short-term loans for buyers who need to close before selling
  • Construction and renovation financing — for projects that traditional lenders won't touch

How to Apply for a CMI Mortgage

CMI works primarily through mortgage brokers rather than direct applications. The process generally looks like this:

  1. Engage a licensed mortgage broker — they have access to CMI and can assess whether you're a fit and negotiate terms on your behalf.
  2. Gather documentation — property details, income evidence (bank statements, T1s if available, business financials), existing mortgage statements if applicable.
  3. Broker submits to CMI — CMI underwrites the deal, orders an appraisal, and provides a commitment letter.
  4. Legal and funding — your lawyer handles the mortgage registration and funds flow at close.

Timelines are generally faster than traditional lenders — experienced borrowers report approvals in days and closes within 1–2 weeks when paperwork is in order.

Exit strategy matters: Any reputable broker or CMI underwriter will want to understand how you plan to exit the private mortgage at the end of the term. Whether you're working to repair credit, build a business track record, or waiting to sell another property — having a plan makes the application stronger and protects you from an unplanned renewal at the same rate indefinitely.

Is a Private Mortgage From CMI Right for You?

A private mortgage through CMI or a similar lender makes sense as a short-term bridge — not as a permanent home financing strategy. The cost is the trade-off for access. If you can qualify at a B lender, do that first. If not, private can get you into a property or prevent a forced sale while you get your financial situation stabilised.

The key is having a clear 12-month plan: improve your credit, build your income paper trail, or pay down enough equity that you qualify conventionally at renewal. Private lending works best when it's a calculated step, not a last resort with no exit strategy.
